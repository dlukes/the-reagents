You inspected the palimpsest under sunlight

This is perhaps the oldest trick in the book for trying to read palimpsests, but it has its drawbacks. Notably, after repeated exposure to direct sunlight, the writing on the manuscript will have a tendency to fade.

You tried to use a chemical reagent on the palimpsest…

… but luckily, a conservator from the Cambridge University Library stopped you in the nick of time! In the past, people loved to experiment with chemical reagents on palimpsests, in the hopes that they might get lucky and magically restore the text that had been written over. Unfortunately, this typically resulted in considerable damage to, or outright destruction of, the palimpsest.

You had a cup of coffee while working…

… and accidentally left a cup stain on the palimpsest! In general, safety precautions while working with such priceless artifacts forbid eating or drinking in your workspace area to prevent accidents of this type. Luckily, your palimpsest is just a virtual copy, so you can magically clean it up with the button in the sidebar.
